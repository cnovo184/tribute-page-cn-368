James Earl Jones (1931 - 2024)

James Earl Jones is an American actor well known for his deep, resonant voice who voiced many roles in movies such as Darth Vader, Mufasa, King Jaffe, Terence Mann and many more!

About James

  1. 1953 - Began his acting career at Ramsdell Theatre in Manistee, Michigan
  2. 1957 - Made his broadway debut as an understudy in the play “The Egghead”
  3. 1964 - First film role in Stanley Kubrick’s “Dr. Strangelove”
  4. 1970 - Won Tony Award for best actor for the play “The Great White Hope”
  5. 1977 - Provided the voice for Darth Vader in “Star Wars”
  6. 1987 - Won two Emmy Awards for “Gabriel's Fire” and “Heat Wave”
  7. 1994 - Voiced Mufasa in Disney’s “The Lion King”
  8. 2009 - Received Screen Actors Guild Life Achievement Award
  9. 2011 - Honered with Academy Honorary Award

Did you know

  • James was self conscious of his voice and dealt with stutter back in high school and didn't speak much.
  • James starred in hit movies like “Coming to America” “Field of Dreams”
  • James voiced Mufasa in both the animated and live-action versions of “The Lion King
